Quick Start Guide

This tool is designed for post-producers, cinematographers, editors, DITs, and content creators who need to visualize aspect ratios, create custom mattes, and simulate sensor crops with mathematical precision.

1. Basic Concepts

There are two primary ways to utilize this tool, depending on your stage of production:

A. Overlay Generator

Design custom aspect ratio guides and export high-resolution transparent overlays compatible with all editing software.

You can set your aspect ratio and resolution, then export a clean, transparent PNG with the matte and frame guides. This is perfect for importing into Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, or Avid as a timeline overlay.

B. Visual Preview

Choose a file from your device to visualize framing.

Drag and drop a still or click to select a file from your computer. This allows you to check compositions, simulate sensor crops, and verify safety areas against your actual footage.

Privacy Note: All processing happens locally in your browser. Your images are never uploaded to a server, ensuring complete privacy for your footage.

Fig 1. Choosing a local file for visual preview.

2. Scaling Modes (Fit, Fill, Crop)

When using an image, you have three ways to visualize your shot within the desired aspect ratio:

  • Scale to Fit: Displays the entire original image. Ideal for checking the full composition.
  • Scale to Fill: Zooms in until the frame is filled. Use the Position controls to reframe your shot (Pan & Tilt) into the desired aspect ratio.
  • Scale to Crop: Physically adjusts the canvas size. The final exported file will have the exact dimensions of the frameline (no frame line or black bars).

3. Canvas Mode

The "Canvas" button allows you to view the full image area without applying any mattes or scaling. Useful for visualizing the total image area beyond the active extraction.

Note: When Canvas Mode is enabled, Scale and Opacity controls are automatically locked as you are viewing the raw 100% image.

4. Secondary Framelines

Need to deliver for Cinema (2.39) and Instagram (9:16) simultaneously? Enable the "Secondary Frameline".

You can customize the color and aspect ratio. Checking "Fit inside main frameline" ensures the secondary guide stays relative to your active crop.

5. Export

Once satisfied, click "Downlaod PNG". The file will download as a high-quality PNG with the matte and guide lines "burned in."
